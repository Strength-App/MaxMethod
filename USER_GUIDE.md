## 1. Getting Started

### Creating an Account
1. Open the app and you'll land on the **Welcome** screen.
2. Tap **Sign Up** to create a new account, or **Log In** if you already have one.
3. Enter your email and a secure password.
4. Verify your details and you'll be taken to onboarding.

### Logging In
- From the Welcome screen, tap **Log In**.
- Enter your email and password.
- Your profile and active program load automatically — you'll resume right where you left off.

---

## 2. Setting Up Your Profile

After signing up, you'll go through a short onboarding flow:

- **Personal info** — name, age, gender, height, bodyweight
- **Training experience** — how long you've been lifting
- **Equipment access** — full gym, home gym, dumbbells only, etc.

You can edit any of this later from **Settings**.

---

## 3. Strength Classification

MaxMethod assesses your starting strength so it can program weights accurately.

### What You'll Enter
- Your **1-rep max** (1RM) for the **Bench Press**, **Squat**, and **Deadlift**.
- If you don't know your 1RM, enter your best estimate based on a recent set.

### What You Get
You'll be classified into one of five tiers based on your bodyweight-relative total:

| Tier | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Beginner** | Just starting out |
| **Novice** | First year of consistent training |
| **Intermediate** | Solid lifting base, steady progress |
| **Advanced** | Years of dedicated training |
| **Elite** | Competition-level strength |

Your tier shapes which exercises and weights MaxMethod assigns going forward. Re-test anytime to update it.

## 4. Setting Your Goals

On the **Goals** page, choose what you're training for:

- **Build muscle** (hypertrophy)
- **Get stronger** (powerlifting / strength)
- **Lose weight** (fat loss + conditioning)
- **General fitness**

You can also set a target bodyweight if weight loss or gain is part of your plan. Goals can be updated anytime — your program will adapt.

## 5. Choosing a Program

### Picking a Program
1. From the **Home** dashboard, tap **Pick New Program**.
2. Browse programs filtered by your goal, experience level, and time commitment.
3. Tap a program to open the **Review Program** page.

### Reviewing Before Starting
The Review page shows you:
- **Length** of the program (weeks)
- **Days per week**
- **Equipment required**
- **Sample week** preview

Tap **Start Program** when you're ready, or back out to keep browsing.

### Switching Programs
- Open **View Program** from the home screen.
- Tap **Change Program** to pick a different one.
- Your old program is saved in your history — you can return to it anytime.

---

## 6. Logging a Workout

### Starting a Session
1. From the home screen, tap **Today's Workout** (or open the **Day** view).
2. You'll see today's exercises with prescribed sets, reps, and target weights.

### Logging Sets
For each exercise:
1. Tap the set you're about to perform.
2. Enter the **weight** and **reps** you actually did.
3. Tap **Save**.

The logger tracks each set individually, so you can adjust on the fly if you under- or over-shoot the target.

### Marking the Day Complete
- Once you've logged every set, tap **Complete Workout**.
- The day is locked in and your progress updates automatically.
- Skipped exercises stay un-logged — you can come back and finish later.

### Quick Sessions
If you don't have a program loaded but still want to train, use **Quick Session** from the home screen to log a free-form workout.

## 7. Customizing Workouts

### Editing a Single Day
On the **Day** view, tap **Edit Day** to:
- Swap one exercise for another
- Add an extra exercise
- Remove an exercise you can't do today

> Edits to a single day only affect *that* day — the rest of your program is untouched.

### Swapping an Exercise Across All Weeks
If you want to permanently replace an exercise (e.g. swap Back Squat for Front Squat for the rest of the program):
1. Open the exercise in the **Day** view.
2. Tap **Swap for All Weeks**.
3. Pick the replacement.

### Building a Custom Workout
From **Custom Workout** you can create your own session from scratch:
- Add exercises from the library
- Set your own sets, reps, and rest times
- Save it for repeat use

## 8. The Exercise Library

The **Exercise Library** is your reference for every movement in the app.

Each exercise card includes:
- **Video demo** — proper form walkthrough
- **Muscles worked**
- **Equipment needed**
- **Difficulty level**

### Custom Exercises
Don't see something you do? Add it yourself:
1. Open the Library and tap **Add Custom Exercise**.
2. Enter the name, category, and equipment.
3. It'll show up alongside the built-in exercises in your customizations.

---

## 9. Tracking Progress & History

### History Page
The **History** page shows every session you've logged:
- Filter by date range, exercise, or program.
- Tap a session to see every set you logged that day.

### Personal Bests
Your **PRs** are tracked automatically. Anytime you hit a new heaviest weight (for any rep range) on an exercise, MaxMethod logs it as a new personal best.

### Analytics Charts
On the home dashboard you'll find:
- **Volume over time** — total weight moved per week
- **Strength trends** — estimated 1RMs across major lifts
- **Workout consistency** — sessions completed per week

Use these to spot plateaus and see when it's time to deload or push harder.

## 10. Settings & Account

From **Settings** you can:
- Update your **personal info** (bodyweight, height, etc.)
- **Change your password**
- Update **goals** or **equipment access**
- Re-take your **strength classification**
- **Log out**

> Keep your bodyweight up to date — it affects weight prescriptions and classification.

## 11. Tips & FAQ

### How often should I update my classification?
Every 8–12 weeks, or after a meaningful change in your lifts. Big PRs = time to retest.

### What if I miss a workout?
Just pick up where you left off. MaxMethod doesn't reshuffle your program — your missed day stays available until you complete it (or skip it manually).

### Can I run two programs at once?
Only one program is **active** at a time, but all past programs are saved in history. You can switch back to an old one whenever you want.

### What if a target weight feels too easy or too hard?
Log what you actually did — MaxMethod uses your real performance to adjust future weights. Over time, the prescribed weights will calibrate to you.

### How do I deload?
Most programs have a deload week built in. If you want an unscheduled deload, edit the day or week to drop your working weights to ~60% and reduce volume.

### My exercise isn't in the library — what do I do?
Add it as a **custom exercise** (see [Section 8](#8-the-exercise-library)). It'll be available for custom workouts and one-day swaps.

If something's not working as expected, check the **Settings** page first — most issues come from out-of-date profile info. For deeper questions, reach out via the contact info in Settings.
